The Core Problem

Recommending alternatives at scale
creates new crowds.

Classic prediction-only systems have a fatal flaw: when a platform sends thousands of users to the same “quiet” alternative, it becomes the next hotspot. The recommendation itself is the source of congestion. Crowdless is the first system built to be aware of its own footprint.

Crowdless AI Engine v2

Three layers. One intelligent system.

A forecast-aware, feedback-aware recommendation system. Each layer builds on the last, creating a closed-loop control policy that adapts in real time.

01
Base Recommender

Demand Prior & Live State

We ingest external crowd forecasts via BestTime combined with live venue occupancy and queue depth. This forms the ground truth — where people are, and where they are about to go.

  • BestTime demand forecasts
  • Live venue occupancy
  • Queue depth signals
02
Signal Aggregator

Platform Feedback Loop

We monitor our own platform’s behavior. How many recommendations were sent to this venue? How many users accepted and are currently navigating there? This closes the loop.

  • Recommendations sent & accepted
  • Users currently en-route
  • Self-induced traffic estimation
03
RL Control Policy

Reinforcement Learning Controller

A learned policy that dynamically adjusts recommendation pressure per venue. It trades off city congestion against user friction — minimizing overflow risk and extra walking simultaneously.

  • Dynamic per-venue penalty adjustment
  • Urban efficiency vs. UX balance
  • Learns from user cost signals

The Control Signals

Three signals. One decision.

For every venue, at every moment, the RL policy computes three core signals and transforms them into a dynamic recommendation penalty.

Demand Pressure

The baseline forecast of how many people want to visit this venue — derived from external priors and historical demand patterns.

High

Overflow Risk

The probability the venue will exceed comfortable capacity — accounting for current demand and the recommendations already in-flight on our platform.

Moderate

Recommendation Saturation

How aggressively this venue is already being recommended to others. When saturation is high, the policy backs off — preserving the alternative’s value.

Low
Policy Output
Demand Pressure + Overflow Risk + Rec. Saturation = Dynamic Penalty → Adjusted Rank

Who Benefits

Built for people.
Designed for cities.

For Travelers
  • No more second-crowd trap

    Get alternatives that are genuinely quiet — not the last place we sent 800 other users to.

  • Friction-aware routing

    The policy minimizes extra walking distance and retries. Your time is a cost signal the AI actively optimizes for.

  • Accurate arrivals

    Arrive at venues that are at the occupancy we predicted — not over-capacity from our own recommendations.

For Cities & Operators
  • Reduced peak concentration

    Visitor flow is distributed across venues and time windows — reducing hotspot congestion by design.

  • Self-correcting system

    The RL policy learns when it over-recommends and automatically backs off. No manual tuning required.

  • Equitable traffic distribution

    Visitor load is shared fairly across local businesses and cultural sites — not funneled to the same handful.
